Places To Visit In Vijayawada
Prakasam Barrage
The Prakasam Barrage stretches 1223.5 m across the Krishna River connecting Vijayawada. The barrage serves also as a road bridge and spans over a lake. The three canals associated with the barrage run through the city of Vijayawada, crossing it and giving it a Venetian appearance.
Rajiv Gandhi Park
Rajiv Gandhi Park, set at the entrance of Vijayawada on the way from Guntur, delights visitors with a lush green environment and a sense of serenity within an urban setting. It is named after the late Prime Minister of India, Rajiv Gandhi. With its meticulous landscaping, this park offers an enchanting experience featuring a mini-zoo, a musical water fountain, a play area for children, and numerous exhibits giving insights into prehistoric creatures, through dinosaur models. The park is maintained by Vijayawada Municipal Corporation and comes with an educative touch, as it houses a mini planetarium and an ornamental fish building showcasing various species of fish.
Undavalli Caves
City of Vijaywada is surrounded by hills and these are incredibly rich in architecture and stunning rock cut structures. The structures are said to be built in 7th or 8th century AD. These awe inspiring arts were inspired by Bhudist crafts. Lot of Hindu deities and murals related to Hinduism can also be found here.
Kolleru Lake
Kolleru Lake is one of the largest freshwater lakes in India located in state of Andhra Pradesh and forms the largest shallow freshwater[3] lake in Asia (with 245 km2 of lake area and 302 km2 of total Ramsar designated wetland).
Sri Durga Malleswara Swamy Varla Devasthanam
The temple of Kanaka Durga the Goddess of power, riches and benevolence and the presiding deity of Vijayawada,Kanakadurga is thronged by lakhs of pilgrims for worship during the “Navarathri” festival which is celebrated with Religious fervor, pomp and festivity. The ancient temple of Kanaka Durga, a top the Indrakeeladri hill on the banks of the Sacred river Krishna in Vijayawada, abounds with legends of historical interest.
